## Introduction

Creating and sharing screenshots helps improve support communication by providing clear visual information. This guide explains how to create screenshots on your computer and upload them directly to support tickets.

## Creating Screenshots

The best workflow for sharing screenshots securely involves creating them locally on your device and uploading them directly to your support ticket via drag and drop. This approach ensures screenshots remain private and are shared securely without relying on external links.

### Windows: Snipping Tool

Windows includes the built-in *Snipping Tool*, which allows capturing a specific area, window, or the entire screen and saving the image locally.

1. Open the **Snipping Tool** by searching for it in the Start menu.
2. Click **New** to start a screenshot.
3. Select the area you want to capture.
4. Save the screenshot to a convenient location on your computer.
5. Upload the saved screenshot file directly to your support ticket by dragging and dropping it into the ticket interface.

### Linux: Screenshot Tool or FlameShot

Most Linux distributions come with a default *Screenshot* tool. Alternatively, third-party tools like *FlameShot* offer advanced features.

1. Open the Screenshot tool or FlameShot.
2. Select the area or window to capture.
3. Save the screenshot locally.
4. Upload the saved screenshot file directly to your support ticket using drag and drop.

## Uploading Screenshots to Support Tickets

After saving the screenshot locally, open your support ticket in the ZAP-Hosting panel or support portal.

- Drag and drop the screenshot file into the ticket message area.
- Wait for the upload to complete.
- Submit the ticket with the attached screenshot.

This method avoids sharing external links and keeps your data secure.

:::info Secure Screenshot Sharing
Avoid sharing screenshots publicly or via third-party upload services. If your screenshot contains sensitive or private information, make sure to blur or redact it before uploading.
:::
